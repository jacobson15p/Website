Diamond Research


I am currently working with Dr. Stanislav Stoupin, a staff scientist at CHESS, on developing improved models for the thermal expansion and thermal conductivity of diamond. Understanding the thermal characteristics is of great importance to many applications; diamond electronics and transistors have become a recent hot area of research, whereas X-ray optics have long made use of diamond monochromators. Using the Einstein model of phonon dispersion, we have been able to create a semi-empirical model for the thermal expansion of diamond that is highly accurate for a wide temperature range (0-3000K) by using a least-squares fitting routine in python. We are also working to improve the most current semi-empirical model for thermal conductivity based on Calloway's model by using the widest range of data available.
