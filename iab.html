IAB Research


Currently I am working with Prof. Carl Franck on investigating the phenomenon of Intraatomic Bremsstrahlung (IAB). IAB is a type of radiation emitted from an inner shell atomic electron as the electron is decelarated by the nucleus. In particular, we are searching for the so-called "infrared divergence", a phenomenon predicted to occur at low energies where the magnitude of radiation is thought to diverge. While the theory of IAB has existed for decades, few experimental studies have been done recently, and many of the previous studies suffer from large uncertainties. Our current work aims to improve the uncertainties of these past experiments to effectively "box-in" the theory.

To measure this effect, we have conducted several X-ray scattering experiments off of atomically-thin Cu films at Cornell's High Energy Synchrotron Source (CHESS). To extract our signal from the background, we use a coincidence method, whereby we correlate events happening in two different detectors to allow us to perform a background subtraction, as the IAB signal is ~1% as strong as the noise. To do this subtraction, I have written several python programs to efficiently sort through billions of events to determine coincidences and then recombine them in a meaningful way. In addition to our IAB work, we are planning on using the coincidence technology we have developed for Condensed-Matter Physics to investigate Non-resonant Inelastic X-ray Scattering (NIXS) in TiO2.
