About Me


Hello! My name is Philip Jacobson - I'm currently a first year PhD student in the Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences department at UC Berkeley. I work in Professor Ming Wu's group where I'm researching novel brain-inspired machine learning systems implemented on photonic hardware. Previously, I completed my Bachelor's degree in physics at Cornell University, where I worked on a wide range of research projects. I spent two years in an Observational Cosmology lab, building a Fourier Transform Spectrometer to use for characterization of anti-reflection coatings on silicon lenses, and also optimizing lens layouts for microwave telescopes. Then, I spent a year and a half working in an Atomic Physics group, where I performed experiments at the Cornell High Energy Synchrotron Source (CHESS) measuring fundamental interaction between x-rays and inner-shell electrons. Also during this time, I worked with a staff scientist at CHESS on developing a novel semi-empirical model for the thermal expansion of diamond. My broad previous experiences have led me to develop an interest across the stack, from the device physics in optical and photonic hardware, to the systems-level design, to the software and algorithms that make it all work. Outside of lab, I enjoy being hiking and camping, watching sports, and exploring! Please feel free to reach out if you'd like to chat!
